Syllabus#

Course Title: Models of Statistical Analysis

Course Duration: 16 weeks (3 hours per week)

Instructor: Professor Alejandra Tabares Pozos

Office Hours: Request an appointment via e-mail

Contact Information: a.tabaresp@uniandes.edu.co - office location ML 622

Course Description: This course serves as an introduction to various models of statistical analysis, their applications, and the interpretation of their results. We will cover a range of topics including descriptive statistics, regression models, hypothesis testing, ANOVA, General Linear Models, and more. This course will provide practical knowledge of statistical models that can be applied in a variety of real-world scenarios.

Learning Objectives: Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Understand the principles behind major statistical models.

  2. Implement these models in statistical software packages.

  3. Interpret the results of these models.

  4. Apply these models to real-world data and make informed decisions.

Assessment Methods:

  1. Assignments: 35%

    1. First: 5% (Due on weeek 6 – Sep 11 - Sep 15 )

    2. Second:15% (Due on week 12 – Oct 23 - Oct 27 )

    3. Third: 15% (Due on week 16 – Nov 20 – Nov 24 )

  2. Midterm Exam: 30% – Sep 15

  3. Final Exam: 35% – – Dic 07

Course Schedule:

Week 1: Introduction to Statistical Analysis

  • Course Introduction

  • Types of data

  • Measures of central tendency and dispersion

Week 2: Probability and Distributions

  • Probability Theory

  • Discrete and Continuous Distributions

Week 3: Hypothesis Testing and Confidence Intervals

  • Null and Alternative Hypotheses

  • Confidence Intervals

  • Error Types

Week 4: Simple Linear Regression - Part I

  • Introduction to regression analysis

  • Simple linear regression model

  • Assumptions and estimation of model parameters.

Week 5: Simple Linear Regression - Part II

  • Hypothesis testing

  • Confidence intervals

  • Prediction intervals

  • Model adequacy checking.

Week 6: Review and Midterm Exam

Week 7: Multiple Linear Regression - Part I

  • Introduction to multiple linear regression

  • Estimation of model parameters

  • Model interpretation.

Week 8: Multiple Linear Regression - Part II

  • Hypothesis testing

  • Confidence intervals

  • Prediction intervals

  • Multicollinearity.

Week 9: Recess week

Week 10: Regression Diagnostics and Remedies

  • Residual analysis

  • Detection and treatment of outliers

  • Transformations for variance stabilization.

Week 11: Regression with Categorical Predictors

  • Dummy variables

  • Interaction effects

Week 12: Logistic Regression - Part I

  • Introduction to logistic regression

  • Estimation of model parameters

  • Model interpretation.

Week 13: Logistic Regression - Part II

  • Hypothesis testing

  • Model selection

  • Prediction and classification

Week 14: Advanced Topics in Regression

  • Poisson regression

  • Cox regression

  • Survival analysis

Week 15: Review Session

  • Key Course Takeaways

  • Q&A and Final Exam Prep

Week 16: Final Exam

Textbook and Readings:

  1. Textbooks:

    • First part of the course: “Applied Statistics and Probability for Engineers” by Douglas C. Montgomery and George C. Runger.

    • Second part of the course: “Introduction to linear regression analysis” by Douglas C. Montgomery, Elizabeth A. Peck, and Vining G. Geoffrey

  2. Additional readings will be assigned as necessary.

Software Requirement: Access to a statistical software package such as Python is required. The primary language used in the course will be Python, but you’re welcome to utilize any other language that you prefer.

Academic Integrity Policy: Academic dishonesty will not be tolerated and will result in a failing grade for the course and the following disciplinar process. All work must be your own. Referencing others’ work must be appropriately cited.

Classroom Etiquette: All students are expected to treat each other with respect and contribute to a positive learning environment. Disruptive or disrespectful behavior will not be tolerated.

Accessibility: If you need accommodations due to a disability, please contact the Register Office. It is important that accommodations are arranged early in the semester.

Note: This syllabus is a guide for the course and is subject to change with advanced notice.
